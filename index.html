Destinations to Inspire

Tanzania Nomad operates across East Africa, crafting meaningful journeys through diverse landscapes, cultures, and wildlife-rich regions.

Tanzania

Tanzania is one of Africa’s most iconic safari destinations, home to vast landscapes and extraordinary wildlife. The Serengeti plains host the legendary Great Migration, while the Ngorongoro Crater shelters one of the highest concentrations of wildlife on Earth. Beyond the savannah, Mount Kilimanjaro rises above the clouds as Africa’s tallest peak, attracting trekkers from around the world. Tanzania also offers coastal beauty, where Zanzibar’s white sand beaches and historic Stone Town provide the perfect ending to an unforgettable East African safari journey.

Visit Tanzania

Kenya

Kenya is the birthplace of the classic African safari. The Maasai Mara’s golden savannahs are famous for dramatic wildlife encounters and the thrilling river crossings of the Great Migration. Beyond the Mara, Kenya reveals remarkable diversity, from the flamingo-filled lakes of the Great Rift Valley to the elephant herds of Amboseli roaming beneath Mount Kilimanjaro. Rich cultural traditions create a deeper connection to the landscapes and heritage that define Kenya’s timeless safari experience.

Visit Kenya

Uganda

Often called the Pearl of Africa, Uganda offers lush landscapes, dramatic waterfalls, and dense rainforests rich with wildlife. The country is globally renowned for its mountain gorilla trekking experiences in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest, where travelers encounter these rare primates in their natural habitat. Uganda’s national parks also reveal tree-climbing lions, expansive savannahs, and the powerful Nile River at Murchison Falls.

Visit Uganda

Rwanda

Rwanda, known as the Land of a Thousand Hills, offers one of Africa’s most refined eco-tourism experiences. Rolling green landscapes stretch across the country, creating dramatic scenery and tranquil beauty. The highlight is gorilla trekking in Volcanoes National Park, where visitors experience a rare and moving encounter with endangered mountain gorillas. Rwanda combines conservation leadership, cultural pride, and pristine nature.

Frequently Asked Questions

Discover answers to common queries about Zanzibar’s enchanting islands, pristine beaches, unique culture, and unforgettable safari adventures. Plan smarter and enjoy every moment of your journey.

What is the best time to visit Tanzania for a safari?
Tanzania offers excellent wildlife viewing throughout the year. The dry season from June to October is considered the best time for safari because animals gather around water sources and vegetation is thinner. For the Great Migration river crossings, many travelers visit the northern Serengeti between July and October.
Do I need a visa to travel to Tanzania?
Most international travelers require a Tanzanian visa. Many nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival, while others must apply in advance. Requirements can change, so travelers should always check the latest immigration guidelines before departure.
Is Tanzania safe for tourists?
Tanzania is generally considered one of Africa's safest safari destinations. Millions of travelers visit every year without issues. As with any destination, visitors should follow local advice, avoid displaying valuables unnecessarily, and use reputable tour operators and accommodations.
How many days should I spend on a Tanzania safari?
Most travelers choose between 5 and 8 days for a Northern Circuit safari covering Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, Tarangire, and Lake Manyara. Shorter safaris are possible, but longer itineraries allow more wildlife viewing and a relaxed travel pace.
Can I combine a safari with Zanzibar beach holidays?
Yes. Combining a Tanzania safari with Zanzibar is one of the most popular travel itineraries. Many visitors enjoy several days of wildlife viewing before flying to Zanzibar for relaxation, beaches, snorkeling, and cultural experiences.
What should I pack for a Tanzania safari?
Recommended items include lightweight neutral-colored clothing, comfortable walking shoes, a hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent, a camera, binoculars, and a light jacket for early morning game drives when temperatures can be cool.
Are children allowed on safari?
Yes. Many lodges and safari camps welcome families and offer family-friendly activities. Some accommodations have minimum age requirements, so it is important to discuss family travel plans when booking your safari.
What currency is accepted in Tanzania?
The Tanzanian Shilling is the official currency. US dollars are widely accepted in the tourism industry, particularly for safari services, park fees, and accommodations. Carrying some local currency is useful for smaller purchases and tips.
Is tipping expected during a safari?
Tipping is customary but always voluntary. Safari guides, camp staff, and porters are commonly tipped as a gesture of appreciation for good service. We provide recommended tipping guidelines before your trip.
Do I need vaccinations before traveling to Tanzania?
Health requirements vary depending on your country of origin and travel history. A Yellow Fever certificate may be required in certain circumstances, and travelers often consult their healthcare provider regarding recommended vaccinations and malaria prevention before visiting Tanzania.
